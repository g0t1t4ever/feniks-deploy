# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

**Primary audience:**
- People struggling with addiction/substance use who are actively seeking treatment — in a vulnerable state, searching for credible help, overwhelmed by choice and uncertainty
- Family members and supporters researching rehabilitation options — concerned, seeking reassurance, evaluating the center's approach and expertise

**Context:** Users arrive via search or referral, often from mobile devices or during a crisis moment. They need to quickly assess whether Feniks is legitimate, effective, and the right fit for their needs.

## Product Purpose

Feniks is a rehabilitation center marketing site that communicates hope, expertise, and compassion to people and families seeking recovery from addiction. The site establishes trust through transparency about the treatment approach, showcases the facility's human expertise, and makes it easy to reach out for help.

## Brand Personality

**Warm with professional grounding.** Feniks balances genuine human care with credible clinical expertise. Not clinical or cold — approachable and personal. Not trendy or superficial — serious about recovery and rooted in proven methods.

**Phoenix metaphor:** The brand embodies renewal and second chances. The visual identity (orange, rising energy) reflects rebirth and hope.

## Anti-references

- **Overly clinical/sterile** — no hospital aesthetic, avoid cold institutional design
- **Trendy/flashy** — no design-trend chasing, no superficiality
- **Overly simplistic** — the site must convey expertise and depth, not strip complexity to nothing

## Design Principles

1. **Earned trust through transparency** — Show the people, methods, and process. Don't hide behind abstractions or jargon.

2. **Human-centered, not institutional** — Emphasize the person leading treatment, the individual approach, the stories of recovery.

3. **Orange as energy and hope** — Color carries emotional weight. Orange is alive, warm, and forward-looking (not corporate blue or clinical white).

4. **Clear next step** — Every section moves users toward action: contact form, phone line, documentation of services. No ambiguity.

5. **Accessible to all literacy levels** — Content must be clear and direct. Many users are in distress; nothing should require multiple reads.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- **WCAG 2.1 AA minimum** — Inclusive color contrast, keyboard navigation, screen reader support
- **Reduce cognitive load** — Clear headings, short paragraphs, visual hierarchy that guides the eye
- **Multilingual readiness** — Site is currently Ukrainian; if expanded, maintain the same warmth and clarity in translations
- **Mobile-first** — Many users will access from phones during a crisis moment
- **Crisis support visible** — 24/7 phone line and anonymous hotline must be prominent, fast to find
